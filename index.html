On May 31st, I left New Haven with a mix of general feeling. I wasn’t sure what happened during the past year. In the PDF that we received, the part that got my attention was “I hope you have a wonderful and productive summer.” So I started to ask to myself: what does it mean be productive? Why should I not be unproductive? Is it that bad?

I was debating about this 'homework'. I felt I was back in elementary school when by the end of the summer you should have finished the book that the teacher gave you. For this reason, I decided that instead of complaining, doing what I think I do better: living my free time. I had a change to talk with old friends, eating things that I never had before, travel in country that I felt in love for first time and see new places.

Today, I decided to open my iPhone gallery and share with you 10 images that probably mostly represent this time of “un-productive” work (which by the way, to me, was really productive).


I’m looking forward to work with you,
